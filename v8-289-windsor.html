V8 289 (Windsor) Engine – The Heart of Classic Mustangs

The V8 289 (Windsor) is one of the most iconic engines in Ford’s history, especially revered among classic Mustang enthusiasts. Produced from 1964 to 1968, this small-block V8 laid the foundation for Ford's performance legacy during the golden era of muscle cars.

Key Highlights:

  • Displacement: 289 cubic inches (4.7 liters)
  • Configuration: Small-block V8
  • Power Output: Varied from 195 hp to 271 hp, depending on carburetion and performance package
  • Torque: Strong low-end torque, perfect for spirited driving

Features and Performance:

The 289 Windsor engine was compact, lightweight, and highly reliable, making it ideal for the sporty Ford Mustang. Its small-block design allowed for excellent balance between performance and fuel efficiency for its time. Enthusiasts loved the smooth power delivery and responsive acceleration, making cruising or racing equally thrilling.

Several variants existed, including the HiPo “K-code” 289, which became legendary among collectors for its enhanced power and performance upgrades, turning the Mustang into a true muscle car icon.

Legacy:

The 289 engine was not just about raw power—it defined the classic Mustang driving experience. Its engineering simplicity and robustness allowed it to become a favorite for aftermarket tuning and modifications. Even today, the V8 289 Windsor is celebrated in restorations and vintage racing, preserving its legacy as a cornerstone of American automotive history.
