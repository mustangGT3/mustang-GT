Mustang GT Engines

A quick look at key engines used in Mustang GT models over the years.

V8 289 (Windsor)

1964–1968 — Early small-block V8 used in classic Mustangs; strong for its era.

V8 302 (5.0)

1968–1973, 1979–1993 — The 302 became an iconic small-block option for performance Mustangs.

V8 351 (Windsor/Cleveland)

1969–1974, later variants — Offered higher displacement and torque for muscle-era models.

5.0 Coyote (V8)

2011–present — Modern 5.0L DOHC V8 known for tuneability and high-revving performance.

5.2 'Voodoo' (V8)

2015–2020 — Flat-plane crank V8 used in high-performance Shelby variants (high-revving).

5.2 'Predator' (V8)

2022–present — Evolved high-performance V8 used in latest Shelby and GT500-level models.

2.3L EcoBoost (I4)

2015–present — Turbocharged four-cylinder option for modern Mustangs, offering efficiency and strong mid-range power.
