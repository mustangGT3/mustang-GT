History & Origins

The Ford Mustang was launched in 1964 by the Ford Motor Company. It was introduced to the public at the World's Fair in New York in April 1964 and quickly became an American automotive icon. The Mustang was conceived as an affordable, sporty car aimed at younger buyers and combined performance with distinctive styling.

Where it began

The Mustang was developed at Ford's headquarters in Dearborn, Michigan. It was built on the Ford Falcon platform to keep costs down and allow quick development. Production began at the Ford plant in Dearborn and later expanded to several other plants as demand surged.
